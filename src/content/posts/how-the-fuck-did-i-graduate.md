When I first stepped foot into my university, the first thing that came to my mind was, _"What will I become after I graduate from here?"_ You know, one of those nerdy freshmen who dream way too big but are too stupid to see reality.

All of my friends were talking about working at Company X, Y, or Z, while others thought they were gonna build the next unicorn startup or some shit.

LMAO... sorry, bitch. It ain't going to happen. Hahaha.

The first semester was alright. Nothing too crazy happened, except I spent way too much time trying to please lecturers, being a fucking people pleaser, and introducing myself to a whole new group of friends all over again. Other than that, nothing much happened.

The second semester all the way until semester five (and I'll tell you why it stops there) was an absolute rollercoaster.

I did two internships outside of my university schedule. Imagine having to work on my laptop while listening to a lecturer talk about something I completely forgot years later. 

And because apparently that wasn't enough suffering, I also joined **Bangkit by Google**. The program converted most of my credits, so technically I only had to show up on campus about two days a week.

Sounds nice, right?

Wrong.

The other five days weren't exactly "days off." They were filled with internships, assignments, meetings, and whatever the fuck decided to happen that week.

So yeah... I "skipped" a lot of classes.

Not because I was sleeping at home like a responsible college slacker.

I was usually somewhere else working.

And you know what's even worse?

The fucking meetings.

I literally had to walk outside the classroom just to attend meetings because one of my lecturers didn't tolerate any distractions during class.

Fucking insane.

On top of those two internships, I also became an assistant lecturer. Because apparently I wasn't busy enough already.

If I remember correctly, I was teaching second-semester students. I did that twice, which means I spent an entire year teaching the same subject.

It was Web Programming with PHP, HTML, JavaScript, MySQL, and God knows what other ancient technologies they were still using. Lmao.

Honestly, being an assistant lecturer was fun. I loved teasing students about their code, roasting their assignments a little, and just messing around with them. It was actually one of my favorite experiences in university.

Anyway...

Fast forward.

I think I was in semester six when I joined a blockchain research team. Yeah, this fancy buzzword that sounds like the name of a Terminator villain.

But honestly?

It's basically just immutable data storage shared across a network.

See? Way less intimidating than people make it sound.

So yeah, I joined the blockchain research team, and we ended up writing a paper. Somehow, our paper got accepted to **ICITACEE 2025** in Semarang.

Fast forward again.

I gave my presentation, did my thing, answered the questions...

...and I passed.

Holy fucking smokes.

That felt amazing.

Then my two friends and I celebrated by grabbing drinks at a bar.

About three months later, I entered the graduation era.

Literally.

I finished university in just three years.

Lmao.

Yeah... I'm too lazy to write the graduation part.

But somehow...

I graduated.

To this day, I still have no fucking idea how.
