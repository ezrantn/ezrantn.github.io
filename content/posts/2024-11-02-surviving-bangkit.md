If I had to describe my experience during the Bangkit program in one word, it would be

**Resilience.**

Bangkit is not for everyone. I know this sounds intimidating, but trust me - the amount of assignments, meetings, Instructor-Led Training (ILT), and other activities can be pretty overwhelming for people who aren't used to it.

It's very fast-paced, and most of the time you have to learn by yourself, which I suppose is the point of online learning. While there are ILT sessions where instructors discuss the material and you can ask questions, the majority of your learning journey is self-directed.

Then how did I manage? Was I able to keep up with Bangkit while still in college?

**Of course not.**

I'm not a robot (though I wish I were) who can process material 24/7. Fortunately, I chose the Cloud Computing learning path, which primarily focuses on cloud services, databases, and APIs - essentially backend development. Since I already had experience with backend development before joining Bangkit, it was **manageable** for me.

"What about the assignments? I heard the deadlines are very tight." Yes, that's correct.

Here's my secret: whenever you have spare time - whether during class breaks, lunch breaks, or before bed - try to work on the assignments. If you're unsure how to approach them, try the Pomodoro technique, which helps maintain focus and keeps you sane.

I must confess - my first guilty pleasure was completing a Bangkit assignment while sitting in my college lecture.

"I'm afraid I won't be able to finish the assignments." Trust me, we all share this fear.

Fortunately, we have mentors! Every group is assigned a mentor who specializes in their learning path. As a Cloud Computing student, my mentor is a cloud expert. Here's an interesting fact: mentors are actually Bangkit alumni, so they understand exactly what we're going through because they've been in our position before.

So whenever you're stuck with an assignment, **feel free** to ask your mentor for help. As my mentor said, "mentor itu harus kalian 'ganggu' terus" (which means "you should keep 'bothering' your mentor"). It sounds funny to me, but it's true!

And that's a wrap...

My journey through Bangkit truly taught me the meaning of **resilience**. It wasn't about being perfect - it was about adapting and finding ways to keep moving forward.

Between juggling college and intensive coursework, I learned that success comes from being resourceful. Sometimes this meant working on assignments during lectures, other times it meant reaching out to mentors for help.

The program was challenging, but it helped me grow both personally and professionally. With the right mindset, good time management, and support from mentors, anyone can develop the resilience needed to succeed in Bangkit.

Keep up the spirit, y'all! Whether you're going through Bangkit right now like me or planning to join in the future, y'all got this!

Much love to y'all! 💞
