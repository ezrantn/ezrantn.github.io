If someone asked me to sum up my Bangkit experience in one word, I’d say:

**Resilience** (fancy way of saying “not giving up when things get crazy”)

Picture this: Google teamed up with Indonesia’s tech giants (you know, Gojek, Tokopedia, Traveloka) and our Ministry of Education to create this intense tech bootcamp. They offer three flavors: **Cloud Computing, Machine Learning, and Mobile Development.**

Not gonna lie - this program isn’t a walk in the park. Between drowning in assignments, jumping from one meeting to another, sitting through Instructor-Led Training (ILT), and trying to keep up with college… let’s just say coffee became my best friend.

Sure, we had ILT sessions where instructors explained stuff, but here’s the real tea: most of the time, you’re on your own. They basically throw you into the deep end and say “swim!”

Did I manage to juggle Bangkit and college perfectly?

**LOL, nope.** I’m only human! 🤷‍♂️ But here’s my secret weapon - I picked Cloud Computing, which is all about cloud services, databases, and APIs. Since I’d already messed around with backend dev before, it wasn’t completely alien to me. Thank goodness for small mercies!

**“But Ezra, what about those crazy deadlines?”** Oh boy, let me tell you! My survival strategy? I became a master of stealing moments - working during class breaks, lunch times, even those drowsy minutes before bed. When motivation was MIA, I’d use this thing called the [Pomodoro](https://todoist.com/productivity-methods/pomodoro-technique) technique (fancy way of saying “work for 25 minutes, then watch TikTok for 5”).

Confession time: I may or may not have done Bangkit assignments during college lectures. Sorry, professors! 🙈

There were definitely times when I stared at assignments thinking “What even is this?” That’s where our mentors came in clutch. Each group gets a mentor who specializes in their path - mine was a cloud computing wizard. The cool part? They’re all Bangkit alumni, so they’ve been in our shoes!

My mentor had this funny saying: “mentor itu harus kalian ‘ganggu’ terus” (basically “bug your mentor non-stop”). And you know what? They meant it!

So what did I learn from all this chaos? Being resilient isn’t about being perfect - it’s about being scrappy and creative. Sometimes that meant sneaking in work during lectures (again, sorry profs!), and other times it meant swallowing my pride and asking for help.

Look, Bangkit is tough, but it’s also pretty amazing. It’s like tech boot camp meets personal growth journey meets caffeine addiction. If you’re in the program now or thinking about joining - you’ve got this! Just remember: nobody’s expecting you to be a superhero. Take it one day at a time, lean on your mentors, and don’t forget to laugh at yourself sometimes.

Keep grinding, folks! You’re doing better than you think!
