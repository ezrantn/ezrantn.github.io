## Finding My Path

Growing up in Indonesia, I wasn't the typical "future programmer" you might imagine. Far from being the smartest kid in class, I was actually known as the class clown - a role that came with its share of dismissal from others. But as life would prove, those early academic labels don't define your future.

"七転び八起き" (Nana korobi ya oki) - "Fall seven times, rise eight." This Japanese proverb has become my life's motto, embodying my journey from an unlikely beginning to finding my true calling in technology. It's a reminder that persistence, not initial success, defines our path.

## The Turning Point

My life took a decisive turn during my second year of college when I landed an internship at [Moladin](https://www.linkedin.com/company/moladin) as a fullstack engineer. It was more than just a job - it was an awakening. For the first time, I felt truly alive, discovering what I wanted to do with my life. The experience was transformative, opening doors to a world I hadn't known existed but immediately felt at home in.

## Academic Evolution

My journey into computer science wasn't driven by childhood dreams or early exposure to programming. Unlike many in this field, I simply jumped in without preconceptions, initially knowing nothing more than the vague concept of "coding." But sometimes, the best decisions are the ones we make by following our intuition.

The romance with coding began in college, where I discovered a unique form of solitude that spoke to me - just me, my mind, and my bugs. This solitary dialogue with code became my sanctuary, a place where I could truly be myself while creating something meaningful.

A significant milestone in my academic journey came when I was selected as an assistant professor for research on Batik Lasem in the Metaverse. This project expanded my horizons beyond pure coding, teaching me about metaverse technologies, payment gateway systems, and the nuanced art of client communication. It was my first real taste of academic achievement and professional recognition.

## The Love of Learning

While traditional academics weren't always my strong suit, English stood out as a subject I could rely on - if only because I knew I'd pass! But my true passion for learning emerged in specific technical domains. Today, my research interests span a fascinating array of fields: backend development, cloud computing, cryptography, distributed systems, and cybersecurity.

Reading has become a crucial part of my growth journey. I find myself drawn to books about programming and psychology, constantly seeking to understand both the technical and human aspects of our field. 

## Professional Identity

Currently pursuing my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science at Soegijapranata Catholic University, I've found my stride in software engineering. Go has become my primary programming language, though I'm versatile with several others. My work focuses on backend development, cloud computing, cryptography, distributed systems, and cybersecurity - areas that challenge me to grow continuously.

## Life Beyond Code

When I'm not immersed in code, you'll find me at the gym or on the basketball court with friends. These activities provide a necessary balance to the mental intensity of programming. I'm also an active contributor to open-source projects and enjoy sharing my knowledge through technical writing.

## Looking Forward

My journey from class clown to software engineer might seem unlikely, but it perfectly illustrates the power of finding one's true passion. As I continue to develop in my field, I remain committed to both technical excellence and personal growth. The path hasn't always been straight, but like my motto suggests, what matters isn't how many times you fall - it's that you keep rising.

The future holds endless possibilities in software engineering, and I'm excited to continue pushing boundaries while staying true to who I am - someone who found his calling in the elegant complexity of code.

## Closing Thoughts

As I reflect on my journey from class clown to software engineer, I'm reminded that life's most beautiful code isn't written in any programming language - it's written in the choices we make, the failures we overcome, and the persistence we maintain. My path wasn't traditional, but that's precisely what makes it uniquely mine.

To those reading this who might feel out of place or uncertain about their direction: remember that sometimes the best programs come from unexpected algorithms. My story isn't about being the smartest in the room - it's about finding what makes you feel alive and pursuing it with unwavering determination.

The world of technology continues to evolve, and so do I. With each new challenge, I hear the whisper of "七転び八起き" reminding me that falling is just part of the journey. What matters is that we rise, again and again, stronger and wiser than before.

As I close this chapter of my story, I look forward to the countless lines of code yet to be written, systems yet to be built, and challenges yet to be overcome. The future is an unwritten program, and I'm excited to keep debugging my way through it, one step at a time.
