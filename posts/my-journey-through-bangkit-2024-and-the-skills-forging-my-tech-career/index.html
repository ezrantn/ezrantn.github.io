My Journey Through Bangkit 2024 and the Skills Forging My Tech Career

Dec 13, 2024
#experience#learning

Background

My nearly four-month journey in Bangkit has finally come to an end. It all started when I was 19 years old and saw my senior join the program. At first, I had no idea what Bangkit was, but after researching, it instantly caught my attention.

Bangkit is a program initiated by major companies like Google, Tokopedia, and Gojek, together with Indonesia’s Ministry of Education, to provide high-quality training in specific areas. It’s one of the most rigorous and transformative tech bootcamps in the country, designed to bridge the gap between academic learning and industry needs.

At the time of writing, Bangkit offers three learning paths:

  • Cloud Computing (my choice)
  • Mobile Development
  • Machine Learning

Each path has its own curriculum and resources:

  • Cloud Computing uses materials from Google Cloud Skill Boost (GCSB) and Dicoding.
  • Machine Learning combines Dicoding and Coursera materials.
  • Mobile Development focuses on Dicoding modules.

The program relies heavily on self-learning, which might seem daunting at first but is manageable with resilience and good time management.

The Learning Experience

The program is no walk in the park. Between the never-ending assignments, attending meetings, Instructor-Led Training (ILT) sessions, and college exams, life felt like a juggling act on steroids. Coffee wasn’t just a beverage; it was my survival tool.

There were nights when I worked on assignments until 2 AM, knowing I had a college exam at 8 AM the next morning. There were days when I attended Bangkit group meetings during class, muting my mic so my lecturer wouldn’t notice. Sometimes, I’d review material during lunch, then prepare for a presentation right after.

One particularly chaotic week stands out: I had a midterm exam, a critical Bangkit project deadline, and a meeting with our mentor—all in the same week. I survived thanks to the Pomodoro technique (25 minutes of focus, 5 minutes of rest… though sometimes I stretched that rest scrolling TikTok).

Was I flawless in managing Bangkit and college? Absolutely not. But the goal was never perfection—it was persistence.

The Capstone Project

The capstone project marks the culmination of the Bangkit journey. Teams from different learning paths collaborate to create a real-world product. My team comprised:

  • 2 Cloud Computing participants
  • 3 Machine Learning participants
  • 2 Mobile Development participants

We brainstormed and built Hyfit, a calorie-tracking app with food-scanning capabilities. The original idea included more features like workout management and meal planning, but due to the tight one-month deadline, we focused on:

  • Authentication
  • Profile management
  • Food scanner functionality

As a Cloud Computing participant, I handled the backend and cloud deployment using:

  • NestJS (chosen over Go for team familiarity)
  • Cloud Run for deployment
  • Cloud SQL (PostgreSQL) for the database
  • Cloud Storage for model deployment

We faced challenges like running out of cloud credits due to configuration issues, but Bangkit provided extra credits upon request.

The Machine Learning team built the image recognition model, while the Mobile team developed the frontend and integrated it with our APIs.

We presented our project twice — a recorded demo and a live peer review. Despite nerves, our preparation paid off, and we successfully delivered our presentation.

Mentorship and Support

Each group in Bangkit is assigned a mentor, usually an alumnus who understands the struggles firsthand. My mentor specialized in Cloud Computing and was always ready to help. Their mantra:

“Mentor itu harus kalian ‘ganggu’ terus” — Don’t hesitate to reach out to your mentor frequently.

I often messaged my mentor late at night with questions, and their patience reminded me that I wasn’t alone.

Reflection

When asked to sum up my Bangkit experience in one word, my answer is simple: resilience — the ability to persevere and adapt when everything seems to pile up all at once.

Bangkit taught me more than technical skills. It taught me adaptability, teamwork, and the value of asking for help. Resilience isn’t about being perfect; it’s about showing up, trying your best, and finding creative ways to keep moving forward — even if that means sneaking in work during lectures or staying up past midnight debugging code.

Bangkit is tough, but it’s also transformative. It’s more than a bootcamp — it’s a journey of self-discovery, growth, and (lots of) caffeine-fueled late nights.

To anyone considering joining Bangkit: take it one day at a time, don’t hesitate to seek help, and celebrate your progress — no matter how small. You’re doing better than you think, and every step forward is worth it.

I’m immensely grateful to my teammates — Renza, Adit, Alvien, Radit, Annaz, and Huda — for their dedication and collaboration. This journey has been unforgettable.

Thank you for reading on my Bangkit journey. Peace out!
